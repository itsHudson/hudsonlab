Role Evolution

From frontline execution to operational leadership

My professional journey started in the retail and food service industry as a cashier and customer service staff member. This early stage introduced me to transactions, customer interaction, and the importance of service quality in shaping customer experience.

Over time, I progressed into broader operational responsibilities, including bakery production, cake decoration, and service support. These roles gave me practical exposure to workflow coordination, product quality, time management, and how multiple departments contribute to a successful business.

As my experience grew, I moved into administrative and operations management responsibilities, supporting outlet coordination, inventory monitoring, and business reporting. Later, as an Outlet Manager, I supervised teams of 10–15 staff members, improved service delivery, supported training, and made more decisions using real operational data.
