Organisation Story

Where practical business understanding was built

My experience at AC Cake House (M) Sdn Bhd represents the strongest operational foundation in my journey. It began with direct customer-facing responsibilities and gradually expanded into production, coordination, outlet operations, and management.

Working across different functions gave me a practical understanding of how business activities connect in real life. I was not only exposed to service delivery, but also to product preparation, quality expectations, inventory visibility, internal coordination, and team supervision.

This organisation shaped how I think about systems today. It taught me that good outcomes are rarely created by one role alone — they come from how people, process, timing, and decision-making work together.

Role Evolution

Four internal stages of growth

Node 01 — First Step Into the Working World My professional journey started in the retail and food service industry. Working as a cashier and customer service staff exposed me to real business operations from day one — handling transactions, supporting customers, and learning how service quality impacts customer experience.
Node 03 — Learning Business From the Ground Up Over the years, I progressed through multiple operational roles including baker, cake decorator, and customer service specialist. Working across different departments gave me hands-on exposure to production, service delivery, and quality control.
Node 04 — Moving Into Operations Management As my experience grew, I transitioned into an operations role where I supported outlet coordination, inventory monitoring, and internal reporting. This was my first exposure to the decision-making side of business operations.
Node 05 — Leading Teams & Driving Performance I later stepped into a leadership role managing outlet operations and supervising teams of up to 10–15 staff members. My responsibilities included staff training, operational planning, performance monitoring, and improving customer experience.

Key Skills Built

Practical strengths developed over time

  • Customer Service and communication in a real customer-facing environment.
  • POS operations and daily workflow awareness.
  • Production and product quality control across operational tasks.
  • Inventory visibility, coordination, and reporting exposure.
  • Team collaboration, time management, and outlet execution discipline.
  • Leadership, staff supervision, and performance-focused thinking.

Why This Phase Matters

The foundation behind later business and technology growth

This part of the journey is important because it created a real-world understanding of how business operations function under pressure. Instead of learning only from theory, I experienced customer expectations, process flow, staffing, service quality, waste control, and team coordination directly.

Later transitions into business education, global support, and technology became more meaningful because this stage already built strong practical awareness. It gave me a systems mindset before I formally entered data and computing.
